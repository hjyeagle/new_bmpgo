On-EIO (Bio Sulfur) Solution for Antibiotic-Free, Sustainable Aquaculture

A proposal for applying Suseong's On-EIO (Bio Sulfur) feed additive across all species — olive flounder, abalone, sea cucumber, and more — with a premium seafood partnership through the National Federation of Fisheries Cooperatives (Suhyup)

Korea's aquaculture industry faces a structural vicious cycle: fish stress and disease outbreaks from high-density farming, antibiotic resistance and residue problems from overuse, marine pollution from feed and waste discharge, and declining consumer trust. Through its proprietary On-EIO (processed sulfur) technology, Suseong Agricultural Corporation offers a solution that simultaneously delivers antibiotic-replacing disease prevention and improved weight gain/feed efficiency.

On-EIO is a highly functional aquaculture feed additive made through a microbial process into ultra-fine (nano- to micro-scale) particles. Unlike conventional mineral sulfur, its toxicity is removed through a proprietary process, allowing safe application to farmed fish and shellfish.

2. Overwhelming Disease-Prevention Effect That Surpasses Antibiotics

In a challenge test artificially infecting juvenile olive flounder with Edwardsiella, survival was found to be better than with OTC, the most widely used antibiotic. It also proved superior not only in growth but in immune and antiviral effects.

Resistance to Low-Salinity Exposure Stress

When exposed for 12 hours to seawater with salinity sharply lowered from 34‰ to 10‰, the nano-sulfur-added group recorded a cumulative mortality rate about 20% lower than commercial compound feed and about 30% lower than commercial Chinese-made feed. This means substantially improved survival (tolerance) against environmental stresses common on farms, such as low salinity and air exposure.

Conclusion

In conclusion, On-EIO is the core solution to break Korea's aquaculture industry out of a structural vicious cycle of high-density farming, antibiotic overuse, and marine pollution. There are three reasons. First, in the juvenile-flounder challenge test, the antibiotic (OTC)-treated group was completely wiped out by day 8, while the processed-sulfur group still had 50% survival at day 10 — confirming disease-prevention power that can replace antibiotics. Second, gains of +11.1pp in weight, +25.5pp in feed efficiency, and +16.1pp in survival for adult olive flounder have been proven through National Institute of Fisheries Science field trials, and the same consistent effect has been reproduced in different species such as abalone and sea cucumber. Third, linking production and distribution with the National Federation of Fisheries Cooperatives makes it possible to simultaneously launch a "Suhyup-Certified" premium brand and secure a nationwide distribution network. We propose swiftly pursuing a pilot rollout through an MOU with the National Federation of Fisheries Cooperatives, joint R&D on new species, and cultivation of the premium brand.
