Global Premium Broiler Backed by International Evidence

Suseong's strategy for industrializing antibiotic-free broiler farming and entering global markets through On-EIO (Bio Sulfur) technology

Korea's broiler industry is now moving beyond productivity-focused mass rearing into a value-conscious phase that considers taste, health, safety, animal welfare, and the environment. Through its unique On-EIO (Bio-Sulfur) technology, Suseong Agricultural Corporation presents an antibiotic-free, high-quality broiler feeding model, and further offers a "K-Sulfur Premium Meat" vision that can compete with global premium brands such as JBS S.A. (Brazil), Tyson Foods (USA), and Poulet de Bresse (France).

On-EIO is an advanced bio-material made by completely removing the toxicity of naturally sourced mineral sulfur (detoxification) through a proprietary high-heat treatment and special microbial fermentation process, converting it into a nano-particle form with high bioavailability. Sulfur has powerful natural detoxifying, immune-boosting, and protein-synthesis effects, but its use has been limited by toxicity (arsenic, lead, etc.). Our processing technology removes this toxicity 100%, completing a "natural antibiotic" alternative that can be safely fed to livestock.

3. Mechanisms and Efficacy of On-EIO in Live Broilers

On-EIO fundamentally improves broiler metabolism, positively affecting every stage of rearing. When dietary sulfur concentration is at 0.15–0.2%, protein synthesis peaks, driving improved weight gain, while it activates beneficial gut bacteria and strengthens tight-junction protein expression, simultaneously improving gut-barrier protection and digestibility.

7. Meat Quality Differentiation: Flavor and Culinary Value (Korea Food Research Institute, KFRI)

In the premium broiler market, the value consumers pay for ultimately comes down to "taste." Analysis by the Korea Food Research Institute (KFRI) proved that broilers fed On-EIO feed are more than a simple protein source — they have a healthy fat structure comparable to olive oil. Sulfur-containing amino acids generate hundreds of aroma compounds through the Maillard reaction during cooking, and broilers fed On-EIO have higher intramuscular methionine content, producing a noticeably richer flavor when cooked.
