On-EIO is an advanced bio-material made by completely removing the toxicity of naturally sourced mineral sulfur (detoxification) through a proprietary high-heat treatment and special microbial fermentation process, converting it into a nano-particle form with high bioavailability. Sulfur has powerful natural detoxifying, immune-boosting, and protein-synthesis effects, but its use has been limited by toxicity (arsenic, lead, etc.). Our processing technology removes this toxicity 100%, completing a "natural antibiotic" alternative that can be safely fed to livestock.

3. Mechanisms and Efficacy of On-EIO in Live Pigs

On-EIO fundamentally improves pig metabolism, positively affecting the entire growth cycle. It boosts protein synthesis by promoting amino-acid metabolism and improves vitality through better energy-metabolism efficiency. In particular, by activating beneficial gut bacteria and suppressing harmful bacteria, it raises digestibility and reduces excretion of undigested protein — a dual effect that fundamentally cuts the ammonia responsible for manure odor at its source.

7. Meat Quality Differentiation: Flavor and Culinary Value

In the premium pork market, competitiveness ultimately comes down to the taste consumers perceive on the tongue. On-EIO acts on pigs' lipid-metabolism pathways to dramatically raise levels of oleic acid, an unsaturated fatty acid. Oleic acid lowers fat's melting point for a soft, melt-in-the-mouth texture and is a key component generating a distinctive savory aroma. According to Suseong's field evidence, On-EIO pigs show oleic acid content of 45–50% versus 38–42% in standard pigs, giving them a fatty-acid profile similar to Spain's Iberico pigs.

Marketing Strategy Linked to Processed Foods and Distribution Channels

On-EIO-fed pork is also a game-changing advantage for meat-processing businesses. Its natural preservative power from antioxidant effects extends the shelf life of chilled meat and processed products, a decisive advantage for maintaining freshness on long-distance exports such as to Hong Kong. It can also add sulfur's functionality to less-preferred cuts such as the ham (rear leg), capturing a health-oriented premium ham market that parents can feed to children with confidence. Setting up dedicated On-EIO pop-up corners in large retailers, and letting consumers experience firsthand that grilling the meat in-store doesn't leave odor on their clothes, can secure immediate purchase conversion and brand loyalty.
