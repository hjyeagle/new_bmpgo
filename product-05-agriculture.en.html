On-EIO (Bio Sulfur) Fertilizer for Sustainable, High-Quality Crop Production

A proposal for applying Suseong's On-EIO (Bio Sulfur) fertilizer across all crops — rice, vegetables, and fruit — with joint field trials through NongHyup

Modern agriculture faces a sulfur (S) deficiency problem caused by chemical-fertilizer-centered fertilization systems. Sulfur is recognized as the "fourth core nutrient" after nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K), and is a key factor that simultaneously improves yield, quality, and disease resistance across all crops — rice, napa cabbage, garlic, onion, strawberry, tomato, and more. Through its proprietary On-EIO fertilizer technology (Patent Nos. 10-1959402 and 10-1968905), Suseong Agricultural Corporation presents a solution that simultaneously improves soil conditions, reduces pesticide dependence, and raises farm income.

3. Sulfur's Biochemical Mechanisms and Effects on Crop Growth

Sulfur is central to protein structural stability and metabolic regulation within plants. It is especially involved in cysteine and methionine synthesis, with about 70% of a plant's sulfur existing in the form of these amino acids. When sulfur is deficient, plants cannot convert absorbed nitrogen into protein even if uptake is sufficient — nitrate accumulates and growth stalls.

Sulfur-Related CompoundKey Function & RoleAgricultural Importance
CysteineForms disulfide bonds, maintains protein structureStrengthens protein stability and functionality
MethionineEssential amino acid, initiates protein synthesisRaises crops' nutritional value (food security)
GlutathioneAntioxidant action, stress resistanceStrengthens tolerance to extreme weather and pests
AllicinFlavor and antibacterial compound in allium vegetablesImproves quality of high-value crops such as garlic and onion

For optimal crop growth in terms of nitrogen-use efficiency (NUE), the nitrogen:sulfur ratio (N:S Ratio) should be maintained at roughly 10:1 to 15:1; when this balance is disrupted, the yield-boosting effect of fertilizer use drops sharply. Sulfur is also a precursor of glutathione and phytochelatins, raising resistance to environmental stresses such as drought, salt damage, and heavy metals.

8. Legumes/Oil Crops and Global Field Evidence
